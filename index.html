The Bioscience Challenge

Freshly harvested Sarawak pepper berries contain high moisture content (~60% to 65% w.b.). To prevent rapid enzymatic browning and toxic mold colonization (e.g., Aspergillus flavus producing harmful aflatoxins), the berries must be dried down to a safe level of ≤12% moisture content as set by the Malaysian Pepper Board.

SFP Field Relevance:

Calculating the instantaneous drying rate (the mathematical derivative $M'(t) = \frac{dM}{dt}$) tells process engineers exactly how fast water is vaporizing. This determines heating energy efficiency and prevents thermal degradation of piperine and volatile oils (which occur if dried too fast or above $70^\circ\text{C}$).

Drying Kinetics & Derivatives

1. Moisture Content Equation (Thin-Layer Model):

M(t) = M_e + (M_0 - M_e)e^{-kt}

2. Instantaneous Drying Rate (First Derivative):

\frac{dM}{dt} = M'(t) = -k(M_0 - M_e)e^{-kt}
$t$: Time (hours)
$M(t)$: Moisture content (%)
$M_0$: Initial moisture content (%)
$M_e$: Equilibrium moisture (%)
$k$: Drying constant ($\text{hr}^{-1}$) based on system thermodynamics

Process Controls

Initial Moisture ($M_0$) 65%
Equilibrium Moisture ($M_e$) 10%
Drying Constant ($k$) 0.45 /hr
